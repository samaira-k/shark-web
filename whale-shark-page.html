The Whale Shark!

COMMON NAME: Whale Shark
SCIENTIFIC NAME: Rhincodon typus
TYPE: Fish
DIET: Carnivore
GROUP NAME: School
AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 70 years
SIZE: 18 to 32.8 feet
WEIGHT: 20.6 tons
SIZE RELATIVE TO A BUS:



Ah, the incredible whale shark! Picture this: it's a bit akin to its oceanic comrade, the basking shark, in its dining habits. How does it feast, you ask? Well, it's pretty fascinating! This behemoth simply opens up its enormous jaws and lets the good stuff flow right in. Scientists have this cool theory—they call it "cross-flow filtration."

It's not just these sharks; some other fish and those colossal whales also pull off this nifty trick. Can you believe it? It's like a grand underwater dining club, with all these remarkable creatures sharing the same savvy feeding strategy. Talk about a jaw-droppingly cool way to dine in the deep blue sea!

Sure, let's dive a little deeper into the whale shark's stylish appearance! Imagine this magnificent creature with its head flattened and a snub-nosed snout sitting right on top of its mouth. What really stands out are these cute little barbels—think of them as tiny whiskers—poking out from its nostrils, giving it a unique touch of charm.

Now, picture its sleek body, painted in shades of gray to brown, adorned with an artistic array of white spots scattered among some elegant vertical and horizontal pale stripes. It's like wearing a fashionable outfit, but in shark form! And guess what? The underside of this fashion-forward swimmer is all decked out in pure white, adding a striking contrast to its topside.

As it glides through the waters, its two dorsal fins hang out towards the back, giving it a distinctive silhouette. And the grand finale? That would be its grandiose tail, sporting a majestic dual-lobed design, like the pièce de résistance of its entire ensemble. It's a head-turner, to say the least!

So definitely a vary distinguished gentleman, right?
The whale shark deserves to be the best shark ever! (yes, i am very biased)
